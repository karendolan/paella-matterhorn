Help Page for the Media Player

This page describes how to use the player page.

We are continually working to improve the quality of your viewing experience. In the meantime, you may find that the audio on some lectures is slightly out of sync with the video. This may be especially noticeable on lectures that feature close-ups of instructors. We will make every effort to improve this in the next release.

Basic usage

The player is designed to display two video windows, with the camera and the presentation view, and some information regarding the lecture. You can navigate through the slides and tabs, but you can also begin viewing the lecture by pressing the play button on top of the video window or in the control bar.

Player display areas

There are three main areas in th player:

  • The video display, where you can see the video of the lecture hall and the video of the presentation device and the player control buttons.
  • The bottom pane, where you will find useful information about this lecture
  • The right pane, where you will find information related to this lecture, like other lectures for this series or lecture suggestions.

You can change the arrangement of those areas using the resize tool. You will find four sizes, from the smallest video window to the fullscreen video area.

Accessibility resources in the player

Some basic functions of the player can be used with the keyboard. Here is a list of the keyboard shortcuts:

ALT+CTRL+P: play-pause

ALT+CTRL+S: pause

ALT+CTRL+M: mute

ALT+CTRL+U: volume up

ALT+CTRL+D: volume down

SPACE: play-pause

UP: volume up

DOWN: volume down

M: mute

Managing views

The view button selects which arrangement between the lecture video and the presentation video is displayed, so you can focus on the most interesting points.

Locating content

The player provides several tools to navigate through the lecture.

The progress bar

The progress bar shows the current playing time. You can click over it to jump to any point in the lecture.

The slide navigation tool

Clicking on the last button you can open the slide navigation tool. There you can move your mouse over the slides and a high resolution slide will be shown over the presentation video.

Click on any slide to go to that point.

The view again tool

The view again plugin goes back 30 seconds into the past, to review any difficult point on the lecture.

Sharing content

The social network plugin

The social network plugin allows publishing a link for this lecture in Facebook or Twitter. Please note that the login requirement for protected videos still applies and the access policy of your network may not allow this option.

Help, Feedback, and Publication Listing

The information button provides three options. The first is a link to this page. The second is a link to a feedback page. The third is a link to the publications available in this format.
